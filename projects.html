Dial – Silent Emergency Response App

Entrepreneurship Individual Pitch
Entrepreneurship Product Development Market Research

Situation

I wanted to create an app concept that meaningfully improves safety for people unable to safely call 911 — including domestic violence victims, students, gig workers, and individuals with disabilities.

Task

Develop a complete startup pitch including problem definition, user research, revenue model, and a business model canvas. The idea needed to be realistic, scalable, and socially impactful.

Action

I built Dial: a silent emergency-alert app that allows users to send a discreet 911 alert with GPS and optional audio/video capture. I created market segmentation, TAM/SAM/SOM analysis, a subscription model for individuals, and a B2G licensing model for police and government agencies.

Result

The pitch received strong feedback for clarity, professionalism, and impact. The project strengthened my skills in problem-framing, market sizing, and business communication.

Remington 870 – Supply Chain Case Study

Operations & Supply Chain Management
Supply Chain Analytics Sourcing Strategy

Situation

For a multi-module supply chain case study, I analyzed the Remington 870 shotgun — a highly recognizable product with a complex component-based supply chain and multiple global supplier inputs.

Task

Map the entire supply chain, evaluate supplier performance, diagnose defects, create procurement KPIs, and propose improvements to quality, sourcing, and inventory planning.

Action

I broke down each major component (receiver, barrel, stock, trigger group, coating) and documented suppliers, cost structures, defect patterns, and delivery performance. I built a supplier scorecard that highlighted a critical issue: a 5% defect rate from Criterion Barrels. I proposed corrective action plans and a performance-based supplier improvement program.

Result

The final case study earned strong praise for clarity, depth, and realism. The scorecard design, global sourcing insights, and quality recommendations were highlighted as standouts.

Reverse Psychology in UX – Bias in Design

UX Design Visual Project
UX Design User Behavior Cognitive Biases

Situation

In my UX class, I was tasked with creating a visual explainer that demonstrated a psychological bias used in design. I selected “Reverse Psychology” because it appears frequently in popular apps.

Task

Research real-world examples, explain the bias clearly, and design a clean, accessible educational piece using CRAP visual design principles.

Action

I built a carousel-style presentation that broke down what reverse psychology is, why it is used, and the difference between ethical persuasion and manipulative dark patterns.

Result

The project strengthened my understanding of ethical UX communication and taught me how to simplify psychological concepts into clean, digestible visual content.
